Looming Clowns
Parsons
Studio 1
2023
For my studio project, I had the opportunity to interview my partner about her experience moving to Canada as a Korean high school student. She shared how she faced difficult times living in a foreign country and ofentimes regretted moving to Canda due to the unfair treatment and racial discrimination she received from her peers. Reflecting on her experience, I asked her to think of a material she thought represent her the best. She answered glass because "it is a hard material in which somedays it is viewed as a strong and durable material, while in other cases, it is easily breakable and fragile. I felt so vulnerable...broken down." Deeply relating to her experience as someone who moved to a foreign country as well, I was inspired to create a piece about how people's gazes oftentimes seem like taunting clowns. Using wires, wooden sticks and glass fragments, I created a spherical bubble that represnts one's comfort zone that is protecting itself using sharp glass edges to keep the dark, negative things from entering. Outside, dark clown figures are smiling around the bubble. When the light shines through the clown figures, its shadows create a daunting feeling that towers over one's safety zone.
