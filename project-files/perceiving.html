Perceiving
Parsons
Studio 2
2024
I feel eyes following me 24/7.


Upon being asked to just sit in my dorm room and note down everything that I see and feel, I mostly felt anxious, nervous, and many other negative emotions. Although this tiny dorm room is my shelter and personal space from everything outside, I have only moved into this room seven months ago and have to share this space with a roommate and 5 other suite mates. Thus, I have never actually felt comfortable and at peace in my share of the room. While researching about the psychology behind this feeling of being watched, I found out that different people feel different ranges of this feelings from just being uncomfortable to actually being able to see visions that scare them. I was intrigued by this wide range of emotions/imaginations, so I wanted to illustrate the extreme vision I see when I feel people's gazes.
Using black and white acrylic paint, I covered a two sided open room without focusing too intensely on the details in order to convey the feeling of uncertainly and unfamiliarity with the space. I then carved out some 'frames' in the walls in which the acrylic painted eyeballs peek through the open hole. To intensify the contrast of the eyeballs being hidden in the light and seen in the dark, I attached string lights around the backside of the frames. When the room becomes dark and the light shining the eyes are turned on, the eyes almost seem like hallucinations that frighten and scare the viewers. The wooden chair in the center of the ground allows the viewers to put themselves down onto the chair and experience the feeling of being watched all around them. The angles of the pupils and the white lines flowing to the legs of the chair all present uneasiness and anxiety to the audience who experience this work. Through my work, I tried to emphasize and visually recreate the feeling I'm surrounded by everyday, hoping people who get similar feelings can empathize and relate to my work.
