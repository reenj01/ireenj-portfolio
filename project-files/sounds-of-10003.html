How can sound be an instance of how time passes? Well, we hear a variety of sounds depending on where we are and the sounds never continue forever. It comes to a stop at a certain point and perhaps makes a new sound. These varieties in the sound and its loudness indicate time passing.


While taking my walk around the block of the 301 Residential Hall, I noticed three consistently distinct sounds: foot steps, car engines, and construction works. I noticed how these sounds interacted with one another over time. I could hear some sounds more clearer and louder than the others. I wanted to illustrate how the sounds [one of the five senses] through visual means and to show how even though the sounds occured simultaneously, they varied from each other at different point in times.
