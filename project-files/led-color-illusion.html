LED Color Illusion
Parsons
Studio 2
2024
As an artist with passion and interest for the tech industry [coding, computer science, technology, and mechanisms], I was motivated to conduct research on how technology can be combined together with art, that results in two seemingly different areas compliment each other through its distinct styles and strengths. Through research, I found that both art and technology had so many similarities. Art is seen and noticed for its freedom, creativity, and open interpretation/imagination while technology can be describe as innovative, immersive, and informative. I saw both as a form to express one's emotions, ideas, creativities, and passions. I wanted to make an interactive media that worked hand in hand with a more traditional form of art, such as an acrylic painting, and experimenting with the RGB and CYMK colors.



Through research, I found out that when CMYK colors are shone onto RGB color paint, each light turns one corresponding light into black. For instance, cyan light on RGB paint changes red to be seen as black, magenta light changes green to black, and yellow light changes blue to black. Then, using an Arduino motherboard, a RGB LED light and a button, I created an interactive light source that changes to each of the CMYK colors when the button is pressed. The first press shows white, then cyan, then magenta, and last yellow. In order to magnify and project the light onto the painting, I crafted a light projector using a magnifying glass and cardboard painted in black acrylic paint. Therefore, with every color change in the light, the RGB acrylic painting is seen as a completely different color pattern. The main goal of this work is to shock and amaze the people through the interactive design, so that more people will come to see the blend of how technology and art can be further innovated and come to be inspired to approach both industries in a new way.
